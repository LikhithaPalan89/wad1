Wikipedia is a free, multilingual, online encyclopedia collaboratively written and maintained by a global community of volunteers (Wikipedians) using wiki software. Launched in 2001, it's the world's largest reference work, available in hundreds of languages, and hosted by the non-profit Wikimedia Foundation, relying on donations for funding. Anyone with internet access can edit articles, aiming to provide free knowledge to everyone, with a focus on verifiability and neutral points of view, despite initial criticisms, it has become a widely used knowledge source.

Key Characteristics

  • Collaborative & Open: Anyone can edit (Anyone can edit almost every page), fostering a massive, volunteer-driven knowledge base.
  • Free & Multilingual: Content is free to use, share, and edit, existing in over 340 languages, with non-English versions comprising most articles.
  • Wiki-Based: Uses wiki software, allowing easy editing and tracking of changes, with a history of every edit.
  • Non-Profit & Donated: Funded by reader donations via the Wikimedia Foundation, not advertising.
  • Largest Reference Work: It's the most extensive encyclopedia ever created, attracting billions of monthly views.

How it Works

  • Volunteers (Wikipedians): A global community of experts and non-experts contribute content.
  • Editing: Users add, correct, or improve articles, adding references to support claims.
  • Policies: Articles adhere to standards like Verifiability, Not Original Research, and Neutral Point of View, though policies can be complex for new users.
  • Structure: Each language has its own wiki, with the English version being the largest.

History & Mission

  • Founded: By Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger in 2001, evolving from an earlier project, Nupedia.
  • Mission: To provide a free, high-quality encyclopedia to every person on the planet in their own language, fostering access to knowledge.
